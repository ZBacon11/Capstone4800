
🛡️ CyberGuard98 (the name is tentative) 
The world is becoming more "cyber" and attacks are more digitized than ever. 
My project, CyberGuard98, is a game designed to help older people (50-65+) stay safe. 
I’m using a retro Windows 98 style to make it feel familiar and less scary for them.
The goal is to teach them about social engineering, phishing, and password strength so they don't get scammed.

🎯 The Vision 
I want to take the "Retro To-Do" code and turn it into a gamified teaching tool.
My vision is very similar to this figma design(https://www.figma.com/make/dKC6asLbv9DeumpeqBxqb2/Phishing-Defense-Module-Design?p=f&t=GGXLBwGNWmCT1iPF-0)
Instead of just "adding tasks," users will complete "missions" to spot fake emails,
or test if their passwords are strong enough to beat a hacker.

💡 Why This Helps
_Raising Awareness_: It helps people by showing them what a real attack looks like in a safe place.

_Stopping Scams_: Older people are targeted the most. In 2023 alone, they lost over $3.4 billion to fraud.

_Real Practice with pattern recognition_: Instead of just reading a flyer, they get to actually "play" through a scenario, 
which helps them remember what to do in real life.
